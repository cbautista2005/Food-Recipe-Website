⋆ Crispy Dinuguan is a Filipino dish made of crispy fried pork pieces served with a rich and savory blood-based gravy, often spiced with chili. ⋆

☆ Ingredients:

  • 1/2 kilogram pork belly (liempo) (ask your butcher to cut your pork belly to 1” strips)
  • 1/4 kilogram pork heart
  • 1/4 kilogram pork liver
  • 1/2 kilogram pork intestine
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 whole white onion, peeled and minced
  • 3 cups of pork blood (strained)
  • 3 pcs. bay leaf
  • 1/2 cup sukang Iloco (you can use cane vinegar)
  • 2 cups Knorr Pork broth (dilute 1 piece Knorr Pork Cube in 2 cups water)
  • 10 pcs. finger chili Knorr Liquid Seasoning salt and pepper to taste

☆ Instructions:

  1. Step 1: Boil pork heart, liver, and intestine in a pot of water until tender. Remove intestine and discard the stock.
  2. Step 2: Slice pork heart and liver into strips and set aside.
  3. Step 3: Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Sauté garlic and onion until brown and translucent.
  4. Step 4: Add pork belly, heart, and liver strips. Sauté briefly, then add Knorr Pork Broth, sukang iloco, and bay leaves. Simmer until pork belly is tender
  5. Step 5: Add pork blood when liquid reduces to ¾. Continue simmering until thickened. Add more broth if needed. Season with Knorr Liquid Seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  6. Step 6: Deep fry pork intestine until golden and crispy. Serve hot!
